History of Banasthali Vidyapith

Early History

Banasthali Vidyapith was founded in 1935 by freedom fighters and educationists Hiralal Shastri and Ratan Shastri. The school was established as a response to the need for girls' education in rural Rajasthan. In its early years, Banasthali Vidyapith was a small boarding school with just a few students.

Growth and Expansion

Over the years, Banasthali Vidyapith grew in size and reputation. In 1943, the school was granted the status of a college, and in 1983, it was granted the status of a deemed university. Today, Banasthali Vidyapith is a fully residential women's university with over 15,000 students enrolled in various undergraduate and postgraduate programs.
